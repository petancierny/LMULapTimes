# Bahrain (WEC) - LMGT3 Track Guide

## Source Coverage
- Sources: https://news.racecontrol.gg/tracks/le-mans-ultimate-bahrain-track-guide/, https://news.racecontrol.gg/tracks/the-2025-track-guide-list-for-le-mans-ultimate/
- Patch: not stated in sources (track guide published 2024-11-08)
- Track layout: 15 turns

## Turn-by-Turn

### Turn 1
- Braking point: Just after the 150m board, where the grass ends and the access road begins.
- Turn-in point: Begin trail braking and turn-in after the 50m board.
- Apex: Cut the inside kerb while keeping outside wheels inside the white line.

### Turn 2
- Braking point: Source needed (guide describes a flat-out kink).
- Turn-in point: From Turn 1 exit, aim for the inside kerb of the left-hand kink.
- Apex: Inside kerb; keep outside wheels inside the white line.

### Turn 3
- Braking point: Source needed.
- Turn-in point: Gentle right-hand curve; keep steering smooth to avoid running wide.
- Apex: Source needed (guide focuses on keeping inside the white line and using the exit kerb).

### Turn 4
- Braking point: Between the access road and the 100m board (roughly halfway).
- Turn-in point: Turn in after the 50m board while trail braking.
- Apex: Lightly touch the inside kerb; the track dips at the apex.

### Turn 5
- Braking point: Brake after the bollard on the left while downshifting to 3rd.
- Turn-in point: Source needed (Turn 5 is a kink feeding Turn 6).
- Apex: Source needed.

### Turn 6
- Braking point: Same braking phase as Turn 5 (after the bollard).
- Turn-in point: Once at target speed, turn in toward the inside apex kerb.
- Apex: Cut the inside kerb while keeping two wheels inside the white line.

### Turn 7
- Braking point: Source needed (guide notes it can be flat-out with a possible lift).
- Turn-in point: Source needed (guide stresses the importance of the turn-in point).
- Apex: Cut inside the kerb to shorten the line and keep inside the white line.

### Turn 8
- Braking point: Start braking at the beginning of the red/white kerb on the left.
- Turn-in point: Aim for a mid-to-late apex so you arrive just after the inside bollard.
- Apex: Mid-to-late apex just after the inside bollard; cut the inside kerb.

### Turn 9
- Braking point: Begin braking as the red/white kerb starts on the inside.
- Turn-in point: Just past the crest, about two car lengths before the solid white line.
- Apex: Stay close to the inside kerb but do not apex it.

### Turn 10
- Braking point: Continue braking from Turn 9 and finish on the outside.
- Turn-in point: Turn in earlier than you think after the final downshift to 1st.
- Apex: Cut the inside kerb without running beyond the red/white kerb.

### Turn 11
- Braking point: Use the dark blue paint patch on the right (about 60-70m).
- Turn-in point: After the 50m board while trailing the brakes.
- Apex: Inside kerb; the guide notes apexing with the outside wheels on the kerb.

### Turn 12
- Braking point: Source needed (guide describes a flat-out right).
- Turn-in point: Turn in at the light pole just before the marshal post.
- Apex: Inside kerb near where the fencing ends on the inside barrier.

### Turn 13
- Braking point: About four car lengths before the Avis board.
- Turn-in point: Turn in while trailing the brakes.
- Apex: Tight to the inside kerb, with throttle before the apex.

### Turn 14
- Braking point: Just before the 100m board and before the solid white line.
- Turn-in point: Turn in after the 50m board while trail braking.
- Apex: Cut the inside kerb, keeping outside wheels just inside the white line.

### Turn 15
- Braking point: Source needed (continuation of Turn 14 exit).
- Turn-in point: Let the car run wide and follow the bend back inward.
- Apex: Source needed.
